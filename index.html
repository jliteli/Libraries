Synopsis

Looking back three decades ago, it is evident that the world has changed drastically. With the boom of technology in the 1990s, most people have become dependent on it. Due to this boom globally, the library systems have had to change drastically and very quickly. With this change came new ideas, new format, new structures for how a library would function in a society. From those effects came new technological advancements, through hardware, software, and digital resources. Gone were the traditional ways and the modern ways made their entrance. However, even with the willing change from some libraries to follow the societal norms of technology and it’s dependency on it, there is a key element library systems are missing. It is a fact that there are few people of the newer generations who know, understand, or could even be bothered with accessing the information and resources libraries have to offer. The next task of the library industry is to make their provided information less confusing by simplifying it, more accessible by showing students how to find things on their own, and lastly more appealing by introducing a social interaction component to keep the technological dependent world’s attention.
